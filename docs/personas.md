# Buyer Personas — Mobilise Robotics

> **Synthetic personas** assembled from public Indian market data, KEENON buyer profiles in comparable markets (China, SEA, UAE), and service-robotics industry reports (NASSCOM, IBEF, JLL, ICRA). **Not based on live customer interviews.**
> Use these to shape website copy, sales scripts, demo playbooks, and email outreach. Validate with real interviews within the first 3 months of launch and update this document.

---

## How to read this document

Each persona has:
- **Title + context** — who they are
- **What they care about** — the KPIs they're measured on
- **Pain points** — what keeps them up at night
- **How they buy** — procurement process in Indian B2B
- **Objections** — what will kill a deal
- **What wins them over** — the message/proof that moves them

At the bottom: a **buying-committee map** showing which personas co-sign which decisions.

## Persona 1 — Vikram Rao, VP Operations, 5-Star Hotel Chain
*Hospitality — primary economic buyer*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Age / tenure** | 42–52 · 18–25 years in hospitality · 4–8 years in current role |
| **Company** | Taj / Oberoi / ITC / Marriott / Hyatt / Leela · 500–2,000 rooms across 8–25 properties |
| **Reports to** | Group COO or CEO |
| **Direct reports** | General Managers, Director F&B, Director Housekeeping, Director Engineering |

### What he cares about (KPIs)

- **Guest satisfaction scores** (tracked via post-stay survey + Revinate/Medallia) — direct link to repeat bookings
- **Cost per occupied room (CPOR)** — labour is ~35% of variable cost
- **Staff turnover in F&B + housekeeping** — 40–60% annually in India, killing margins
- **RevPAR** (Revenue per Available Room) — indirect
- **Time-to-service** on room service (industry benchmark: 15 min, best-in-class 8)

### Pain points (what keeps Vikram up at night)

1. **Staffing shortage is chronic.** Post-COVID, hotel F&B staff won't come back at old wages. A 300-room 5-star hotel is short ~25 F&B runners daily.
2. **Room-service margins are getting crushed** — increasing tips + hazard pay + meal allowances.
3. **Guests complain about slow delivery** in the post-aggregator era (Swiggy/Zomato set their expectation to 30 min).
4. **Housekeeping cart deliveries** (amenities, linens, room moves) eat his GRAs' time — takes them away from revenue-generating tasks.
5. **Peak-hour chaos** during wedding seasons and conferences — ad-hoc staffing breaks SLAs.
6. **Owner ROI pressure.** Asset-light brands (Marriott/Hyatt) are franchised. The operator needs to prove every capex.

### How he buys

- **Budget authority**: ₹50 L–2 Cr without CEO sign-off; larger needs board approval
- **Procurement gate**: Requires 3 quotes, a technical-commercial evaluation, a site POC (usually at the flagship property)
- **Timeline**: Discovery → POC → full contract = 6–12 months
- **Decision committee**: VP Ops (economic buyer) + GM flagship property (champion/user) + IT Director (tech gate) + CFO (budget gate)
- **Procurement risk appetite**: Low. Needs named peer references before greenlighting.
- **Payment terms**: 30/30/40 (signing / go-live / 90-day acceptance)

### Objections

- *"Our guests will think it's gimmicky."* → counter with brand partners using it (Marriott Singapore, Intercontinental Bangkok, Jumeirah Dubai)
- *"What if it breaks during Diwali/wedding season?"* → SLA + spare parts in India + phone support in IST + backup robot at cost
- *"My union won't accept layoffs."* → this augments, doesn't replace. Runners get redeployed to guest-facing roles.
- *"The Wi-Fi is shit in some areas."* → show offline operation mode; demo on 3G
- *"Can my IT team manage this?"* → self-managing; KEENON app + 1-day training

### What wins him over

- **Peer case study from a similar Indian chain** (critical — he doesn't care what it does in Shenzhen)
- **ROI calculator** showing payback under 18 months on labour costs alone
- **24-hour SLA + spare parts in Faridabad** (within trucking distance of his northern properties)
- **Pilot deployment at the flagship** — no upfront commitment, just a paid pilot
- **Calibrated claim**: "increases room-service throughput 40%, reduces labour dependency on unreliable staff, pays back in 14 months"

### Messaging hooks

- *"Staff shortage-proof your room service."*
- *"When your F&B runners don't show up, the T9 does."*
- *"Pays back in 14 months on labour alone — and guests remember it forever."*

## Persona 2 — Dr. Anita Menon, COO, Tertiary-Care Hospital Network
*Healthcare — primary economic buyer*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Age / tenure** | 45–55 · Medical background (MBBS/MD) + MBA (usually IIM/ISB) · 3–7 years in current role |
| **Company** | Apollo / Fortis / Manipal / Max / Medanta / Narayana Health · 300–1,500 beds across 5–20 hospitals |
| **Reports to** | Group CEO or MD |
| **Direct reports** | Hospital Directors, CMO, Head of Nursing, Head of Biomedical Engineering, Head of Facilities |

### What she cares about (KPIs)

- **NABH/JCI accreditation** — non-negotiable. Every operational decision filtered through this lens.
- **Hospital-acquired infection rate (HAI)** — target <1.5%
- **Average length of stay (ALOS)** — proxy for operational efficiency
- **Nurse-to-patient ratio** (1:6 in wards, 1:1 in ICU) — and the burnout from understaffing
- **Turnaround time** (ED → diagnostic → bed)
- **Logistics cost as % of revenue** (medicine, samples, meals, linens)

### Pain points

1. **Nurses spend ~90 minutes per shift on logistics** (running meds, collecting samples, delivering results). She pays a ₹55K/month nurse to do a ₹18K/month ward-boy's work.
2. **Ward-boys are the bottleneck.** Every hospital in India is critically short. Union reps block outsourcing.
3. **Cross-contamination risk** is always one outbreak away from losing accreditation.
4. **Pharmacy dispensing errors** when medicines are hand-carried (wrong ward, delayed, stolen).
5. **Night shifts are nightmare** — logistics halts 11 PM–6 AM, crisis management only.
6. **Peak-hour ER chaos** — blood samples to the lab, medicines from central pharmacy, the wait kills outcomes.
7. **Medical tourism** (Chennai/Bangalore) patients expect hotel-grade service; Indian hospitals struggle to deliver it.

### How she buys

- **Budget authority**: ₹25 L–1 Cr herself; larger = board capex committee
- **Procurement gate**: 
  - Clinical evaluation by CMO + Infection Control Committee (HAI risk)
  - Operational evaluation by Head of Facilities
  - Financial by CFO (ROI + payback)
  - Governance: NABH documentation review
- **Timeline**: 9–18 months. Hospitals are slow.
- **Site POC**: Mandatory. Usually on one ward for 60–90 days.
- **Payment terms**: 25/25/50 with 1-year warranty + AMC

### Objections

- *"Infection control hasn't signed off."* → dedicated HAI decontamination protocol, UV-C package, wipe-down SOP
- *"What about HIPAA-equivalent data?"* → robot doesn't store patient data; deliveries are item-level only
- *"Our floor layouts are irregular and old."* → SLAM navigation handles dynamic environments; show case studies from 1970s-era hospitals
- *"What if it delivers the wrong medicine?"* → barcode scanning at both ends; password-protected compartments; audit trail
- *"Nurses union will push back."* → this isn't replacement, it's reclaiming bedside time

### What wins her over

- **Indian hospital case study** with named HAI reduction and ALOS metrics
- **NABH-ready documentation kit** (SOPs, training manuals, infection-control protocol)
- **24/7 on-call biomedical engineering support** in IST
- **Dedicated hospital SKU** with UV-C, barcode scanning, password compartments
- **Peer testimonial from another COO** (her most-trusted signal)

### Messaging hooks

- *"Give your ₹55K nurses their time back for patient care."*
- *"NABH-compliant logistics. Zero cross-contamination. Audit-trail every delivery."*
- *"One T3 replaces 3 ward-boys per shift — and never calls in sick."*

## Persona 3 — Rohit Agarwal, Director of Operations, Restaurant Chain
*F&B — primary economic buyer*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Age / tenure** | 35–45 · Started as restaurant manager, rose through ops · 5–10 years in F&B |
| **Company** | Barbeque Nation / Mainland China / Haldiram's / Wow Momo / Chaayos / Social · 40–300 outlets |
| **Reports to** | CEO or COO |
| **Direct reports** | Regional Ops Heads, Training Head, Compliance/Audit Head |

### What he cares about (KPIs)

- **AUV** (Average Unit Volume) — revenue per outlet
- **Table turns per meal period** — key P&L lever
- **Labour % of revenue** — target 18–22%, India often 25%+
- **Guest satisfaction + Google/Zomato ratings**
- **Staff attrition** — industry avg 120% annually
- **Speed of service** (SOS) — critical at lunch peak

### Pain points

1. **Can't hire enough waitstaff.** Post-COVID, the QSR labor pool shrank 30%. Wages up 40% but headcount down.
2. **Attrition is brutal** — training a waiter for 3 weeks, losing them to Swiggy for delivery work in week 4.
3. **Lunch peak (12:30–2 PM)** is unstaffable in Tier-1 cities. He's over-staffing by 25% to handle the rush and wasting it off-peak.
4. **Customer complaints** are 60% about speed — "where's my order", "forgot my dish", "took 20 min to get water"
5. **Covers per waiter** is trending DOWN while wages trend UP — margin compression
6. **Fine-dining brands under pressure** to justify the premium when staff are overstretched

### How he buys

- **Budget authority**: ₹10–75 L per outlet; bulk orders via corporate office
- **Procurement gate**: Pilot at 1 outlet (flagship), evaluate on 6 KPIs (SOS, guest score, cover/hr, staff satisfaction, incident count, ROI), then roll to 5, then 20
- **Timeline**: 3–9 months — faster than hotels/hospitals
- **Decision committee**: Director Ops + Franchise Owner (if franchised) + Marketing Head (for the novelty/PR angle) + CFO
- **Payment terms**: 50/50 or operating lease
- **Unique factor**: PR value. Restaurant chains are media-sensitive. A T10 at a flagship is a marketing win.

### Objections

- *"Waiters still need to do order-taking and recommendation."* → T10 serves dishes; waiters do relationship + upsell. Complementary not substitute.
- *"Will the robot block the aisle in peak hour?"* → show T8 (55cm narrow aisle) and T11 (49cm)
- *"What if a customer trips on it?"* → stereo vision + 3D obstacle avoidance + liability insurance clause
- *"My QSR doesn't need a ₹8L robot to deliver a ₹180 biryani."* → agreed. Target: fine-casual + premium fine-dining. Not street-price QSR.
- *"Uncle Chun's tried it in Bangalore, sent it back."* → context-specific; investigate root cause; offer guarantee

### What wins him over

- **Immediate ROI** on a single outlet within 12–14 months (not 18+ like hospitality)
- **PR angle** — KEENON + brand = press coverage
- **Demo lunch** at his flagship outlet — not a boardroom deck
- **Staff training module** that turns servers into T10 operators (elevates, doesn't threaten)
- **Volume pricing** (he'll buy 20 if pilot succeeds)

### Messaging hooks

- *"Fix your lunch peak without hiring 15 more waiters you can't find."*
- *"Waiters sell. Robots run. Margins recover."*
- *"The flagship gets press. The rest of your chain gets profit."*

## Persona 4 — Kavita Sharma, GM — Mall Operations, Real Estate Developer
*Retail (property-side) — primary economic buyer*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Age / tenure** | 38–48 · Hotel/retail facilities background · 3–6 years in current role |
| **Company** | DLF / Phoenix / Inorbit / High Street Phoenix / Brigade / Prestige · 500K–2M sq ft premium malls |
| **Reports to** | Asset Head / Property COO |
| **Direct reports** | Facility Manager, Security Head, Housekeeping Contractor, Tenant Relations |

### What she cares about (KPIs)

- **Footfall conversion** — shoppers per day → transaction count
- **Anchor tenant renewal rate** — signal of mall health
- **Avg dwell time** — longer = more spend
- **NPS (mall + tenant)** — cleanliness is a top-3 driver
- **CAM (common area maintenance) costs** — what tenants pay, what she controls
- **Security incident count** — slips, trips, theft
- **Energy + utilities costs** — sustainability pressure

### Pain points

1. **Housekeeping is outsourced and inconsistent.** Contractor churn. Quality issues.
2. **Night cleaning window is 2 AM–6 AM.** Labor for night shifts in India is expensive and unreliable.
3. **Spills during mall hours** are a liability nightmare. SLA is 5 min; actual 15–30 min.
4. **Tenant complaints** about cleanliness eat her credibility.
5. **Sustainability pressure** — LEED/IGBC certifications require operational efficiency metrics
6. **Wayfinding is a mess.** She spends ₹25 L/yr on signage that customers still complain about.
7. **ESG reporting** — developer wants visible tech/sustainability story for investor decks

### How she buys

- **Budget authority**: ₹30 L–1 Cr (out of CAM budget); larger needs asset committee
- **Procurement gate**: Pilot in a low-traffic area (basement, 2nd-floor corridor), scale to full mall if metrics hit
- **Timeline**: 6–12 months
- **Decision committee**: GM Mall Ops + Property CFO + Marketing Head + ESG/Sustainability Head
- **Payment terms**: Often operating lease — CAM recovery model preferred

### Objections

- *"Tenants pay CAM; will they see the value?"* → cleaner mall = higher dwell time = higher sales = tenant retention
- *"Insurance on robot in crowded mall?"* → standard commercial liability, KEENON certified
- *"What about water refill during mall hours?"* → scheduled overnight refills at dock
- *"Sephora guests will bump into it."* → stereo vision + deceleration profile tuned for crowd density
- *"Can it handle the scale?"* → C55 specced for 2,376 sqm/hr

### What wins her over

- **Mall-ready SKU**: C40 or C55 with high-capacity water tanks, quiet operation, dock-and-refill workflow
- **ESG story** — marketing collateral she can use in investor decks
- **Operating lease** — moves capex to opex, easy CFO approval
- **Named reference** from another premium mall in India (critical)
- **Pilot in 2nd-floor corridor** — low-risk, measurable

### Messaging hooks

- *"Marble-clean at 3 AM. Marketing win at 10 AM. ESG story at AGM."*
- *"One C55 covers 1M sq ft on one battery. No night-shift labor, no union, no excuses."*
- *"Your tenants want cleaner floors. The C55 delivers. CAM recovery justifies the lease."*

## Persona 5 — Capt. Sanjay Malhotra, GM Operations — Airport
*Aviation — primary economic buyer (longer sales cycle)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Age / tenure** | 48–58 · Aviation/Defence background (often retired IAF/Army) · 5–10 years in airport ops |
| **Company** | GMR (DIAL, BIAL, GHIAL) / Adani (MIAL, Navi Mumbai, 6 other airports) / AAI |
| **Reports to** | CEO of airport SPV |
| **Direct reports** | Terminal Heads, Ground Ops Head, Commercial Head (retail/F&B), Security Head |

### What he cares about (KPIs)

- **ACI passenger satisfaction index** — the benchmark (Mumbai/Delhi: world-class; others catching up)
- **Non-aeronautical revenue per pax** — retail, F&B, lounge revenue — increasingly critical
- **Terminal turnover time** — passenger flow efficiency
- **Accessibility compliance** — CAR Section 3 Series M Part I + DGCA
- **Operational resilience** (weather disruption, pax surge)

### Pain points

1. **T3 terminal at IGI is a city-sized facility** (52K sqm terminal per terminal). Cleaning is a 24/7 operation requiring 400+ staff.
2. **Passenger volume is rocketing** — India aviation growing 12–18% YoY. Infrastructure and ops can't keep up.
3. **Wayfinding is broken** — multilingual tourists, unfamiliar terminals, signage fatigue
4. **PRM (Persons with Reduced Mobility) support** is labor-intensive and under-delivered
5. **Non-aero revenue pressure** — commercial spaces need to convert better
6. **Sustainability reporting** — airports have aggressive ESG targets
7. **Terrorism + security sensitivity** — any new system goes through BCAS security review

### How he buys

- **Budget authority**: ₹2–10 Cr with board sign-off
- **Procurement gate**: 
  - **BCAS security clearance** — mandatory, takes 3–6 months
  - CISF operational compatibility review
  - Airport vendor registration — 1 year lead time typically
  - Pilot in a non-secure area (retail concourse, arrival hall)
- **Timeline**: 12–24 months
- **Decision committee**: GM Ops + Terminal Head + BCAS liaison + Commercial Head + CFO + Legal/Procurement
- **Payment terms**: Often 20/60/20 milestone-based
- **Unique factor**: Security + accessibility + passenger experience trumps cost

### Objections

- *"Will BCAS clear this?"* → we have the documentation kit; happy to support clearance process
- *"Can you handle airport WAN/Wi-Fi?"* → offline operation mode; no cloud dependency needed
- *"What about peak chaos — 15,000 pax in Terminal 3 at once?"* → show Changi/Incheon deployments
- *"We're an AAI airport, not DIAL/Adani. Different procurement."* → we can work GeM, we have CIN + MSME
- *"Capex pressure from regulator."* → operating lease or revenue-share model available

### What wins him over

- **Airport-grade SKU** — service robots + cleaners designed for 24/7, high-traffic, multilingual pax
- **BCAS-ready security documentation** (VA/PT reports, source code escrow for India, data residency commitment)
- **Accessibility-compliant** (CAR Section 3 Series M)
- **Peer airport case study** (not yet available in India — a foot in the door here is huge)
- **Multi-year service agreement** with dedicated India team

### Messaging hooks

- *"Airport-grade reliability. BCAS-ready documentation. Operates offline."*
- *"From check-in to gate, let robots do the marathon. Your staff focus on the exceptions."*
- *"The first Indian airport deployment. Be the reference the industry cites."*

## Persona 6 — Priya Krishnan, Head of Workplace Experience, IT Park / Enterprise
*Corporate — primary economic buyer, but committee-heavy*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Age / tenure** | 30–42 · HR/facilities/admin background · 2–5 years in current role |
| **Company** | TCS / Infosys / Wipro / Flipkart / Paytm / Freshworks / BYJU'S · 500–10,000 employees per campus |
| **Reports to** | VP HR or CFO |
| **Direct reports** | Facilities Manager, Security Contractor, F&B Contractor, Space Planner |

### What she cares about (KPIs)

- **Employee satisfaction** (eNPS, Glassdoor reviews)
- **Return-to-office attendance** — post-COVID pressure
- **Cost per employee per month (workplace)** — cafeteria, facilities, security
- **Incident rate** (slips, trips, injuries)
- **Diversity & accessibility compliance**
- **Sustainability metrics** — LEED / IGBC / internal ESG reporting

### Pain points

1. **Bringing employees back to office** is the #1 board-level ask. Workplace amenities matter.
2. **Cafeteria wait times** during 12:30–2 PM lunch rush are brutal. Employees skip office food, order Swiggy, leave campus, don't return.
3. **Reception/visitor management** is manual and slow.
4. **Cleanliness during work hours** is a visible employee gripe.
5. **HR fatigue** — every quarter new initiative to "enhance workplace experience"
6. **CFO pushback** on workplace spend — COVID-era cost-cutting still haunts budgets
7. **Tech company, anti-tech workplace** — visible contradiction during investor visits

### How she buys

- **Budget authority**: ₹15–50 L; larger = CFO + CHRO approval
- **Procurement gate**: 
  - IT security review (data residency, network access)
  - Facilities ops compatibility
  - CFO P&L review
  - Employee communication plan (CHRO)
- **Timeline**: 3–9 months (fastest of these industries)
- **Decision committee**: Head Workplace Experience + CHRO + CFO + IT Head + Facilities
- **Payment terms**: Operating lease preferred (opex > capex for tech cos)
- **Unique factor**: Tech companies love the visible tech story. Easier sell at Freshworks than at legacy enterprise.

### Objections

- *"Our IT team will want source code + pen test."* → deliverable
- *"Will it interfere with our security controlled zones?"* → zone restrictions configurable
- *"Do employees want this, or is it HR theater?"* → employee survey shows cafeteria wait is top-3 workplace complaint
- *"What if WFH-ers feel even more FOMO?"* → RTO-positive narrative
- *"Office is leased; what if we relocate?"* → transportable; 1-day re-deploy

### What wins her over

- **Employee-experience framing** — not cost-cutting story
- **Demo day on campus** — let employees see it, tweet it, feel the future
- **Fast pilot** — 8-week turnaround to proof
- **Integration with workplace apps** (Condeco, Robin, Envoy)
- **CFO ROI on labor** + **CHRO story on "workplace of the future"** (dual-champion)

### Messaging hooks

- *"Bring employees back. Make the office feel like the future."*
- *"Cut cafeteria wait time from 18 minutes to 6. Give them a reason to stay on campus."*
- *"Your company's tagline is 'we build tech.' Your office should look like it."*

## Buying-committee map

Who signs off on what across the 6 industries:

| Persona | Economic buyer | Technical gate | Financial gate | Champion / user |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hospitality (Vikram)** | VP Ops ✓ | IT Director | CFO | GM flagship property |
| **Healthcare (Dr. Anita)** | COO ✓ | CMO + Infection Control | CFO | Head of Nursing |
| **F&B (Rohit)** | Director Ops ✓ | Franchise Owner | CFO | Outlet Manager |
| **Retail (Kavita)** | GM Mall Ops ✓ | Facilities Manager | CFO (property) | Tenant Relations |
| **Aviation (Capt. Sanjay)** | GM Ops ✓ | BCAS liaison + IT | CFO airport SPV | Terminal Head |
| **Corporate (Priya)** | Head Workplace ✓ | IT Head | CFO + CHRO | Facilities Manager |

**Pattern across all industries**: 4–6 person committee, 3–12 month cycles, site-POC is near-universal.

**What this means for the website**:
- Every product page should be readable by the **technical gate** (specs) AND the **economic buyer** (ROI narrative) AND the **champion** (day-to-day use case)
- The contact form should qualify which persona is filling it out → routing to the right sales playbook
- Case studies should be *industry-specific* (a hospital COO doesn't care about a hotel case)

## Cross-persona insights

### Universal pain points in India
1. **Labor scarcity** — post-COVID, every industry short-staffed
2. **Peak-hour chaos** — under-staffed for rush, over-staffed off-peak
3. **Wage inflation** — labor cost up 25–40% vs 2019
4. **Attrition cost** — training + lost productivity
5. **Trust in new tech** — needs peer proof before budget approval

### Universal objections
1. *"Show me an Indian reference, not Shenzhen."*
2. *"What about spare parts / service in India?"*
3. *"Will the union accept this?"*
4. *"What's the ROI in MONTHS, not abstract %?"*

### Universal wins
1. **Peer case study** beats spec sheet every time
2. **Paid pilot** beats demo in a showroom
3. **SLA with teeth** (spare parts in India, 24-hr response, warranty) beats price discount
4. **Operating lease** beats capex on CFO desk
