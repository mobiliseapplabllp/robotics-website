# Mobilise Robotics — Business Roadmap

> A Now / Next / Later view of the **company** (Mobilise App Lab Limited — KEENON's India partner). Scope: products, markets, customers, operations. See `website-roadmap.md` for marketing-site work.

> Framing assumption: Mobilise today is in early go-to-market. First customers are landing, the KEENON partnership is signed, but "product-market fit in India" is still hypothesis, not proof. This roadmap assumes a 12–18 month view to get to **50+ customers, ₹25–40 Cr run-rate, and category leadership for service robots in India**.

---

## Company north star

> **"Be the default partner for service robots in India across hospitality, healthcare, and commercial — with deployments, support, and trust no competitor can match."**

Three things this north star implies:
1. **Breadth of use cases** — not just delivery robots, not just one industry
2. **Local ownership** — the India Go-To-Market is *ours*, not KEENON's Chinese team remote
3. **Services, not just reselling** — we train, integrate, and service. Defensible moat.

## What we're deliberately NOT doing (at least for now)

Explicit anti-goals sharpen focus:

- ❌ **Consumer / residential robots** — out of scope. We're B2B.
- ❌ **Building our own robot hardware** — we're distribution + services on KEENON platform
- ❌ **Humanoid / LLM-integrated robots** (XMAN etc.) — not our initial thesis
- ❌ **ROTN (robot-on-the-network) / cloud-first model** — we sell hardware + service, not SaaS
- ❌ **Drone delivery** — different regulatory world (DGCA), different tech stack
- ❌ **Government-heavy sales motion** — slow, political, unpredictable; not our ICP
- ❌ **Expansion to MEA or EU** — too early; India is 1-billion-person TAM

## Risks & how we're mitigating them

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| KEENON partnership terms change | Low | Catastrophic | Contract lock-in; build Mobilise-owned customer relationships; secondary OEM later |
| Competitor (Chinese direct / other reseller) undercuts on price | Medium | High | Compete on service tier + India-local spare parts + credibility, not price |
| Indian hospitality downturn (macro recession) | Low-Medium | High | Industry diversification (Healthcare + Corporate are less cyclical) |
| Labor unions / HR law block deployments | Medium | Medium | Position as augmentation; draft HR playbooks for customers |
| Regulatory uncertainty (robotics-specific) | Low | Medium | Monitor NITI Aayog / MeitY signals; engage proactively |
| Talent: can't hire enough field engineers | High | Medium | Start training academy now; certification program |
| Customer concentration risk (one big account = 30%+ revenue) | Medium | High | Diversify across industries deliberately |
