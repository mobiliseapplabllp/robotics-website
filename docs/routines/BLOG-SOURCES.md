# Citable source whitelist

Facts and numbers in blog posts may only be cited from sources on this list. If a source isn't here and the fact matters, remove the fact or open an issue to expand this list.

Routines that generate content read this file and will refuse to ship a claim that can't be traced to an entry below.

---

## Tier 1 — product facts, specs, certifications

Use for: Keenon product capabilities, model numbers, certifications, awards, partnership announcements.

- keenon.com
- keenon.com/en
- Keenon press releases on prnewswire.com, globenewswire.com, businesswire.com
- Official Keenon distributor announcement pages (verify authenticity before adding)

## Tier 2 — industry data, market size, benchmarks

Use for: market size numbers, CAGR forecasts, industry adoption statistics, competitive landscape framing.

- IDC reports (cite report title and year)
- Gartner (cite report title and year)
- NASSCOM publications (nasscom.in)
- Statista — India-specific datasets only
- IBEF — India Brand Equity Foundation (ibef.org)
- RBI publications (rbi.org.in)
- Ministry of Commerce and Industry (commerce.gov.in)
- FAITH — Federation of Associations in Indian Tourism & Hospitality
- FHRAI — Federation of Hotel & Restaurant Associations of India (fhrai.com)
- NRAI — National Restaurant Association of India (nrai.org)
- FICCI (ficci.in)
- CII — Confederation of Indian Industry (cii.in)
- ASSOCHAM (assocham.org)
- NITI Aayog reports (niti.gov.in)

## Tier 3 — regulatory and standards

Use for: certifications, compliance, HSN codes, GST rates, import duties.

- bis.gov.in — BIS certifications
- fssai.gov.in — FSSAI
- nabh.co — hospital accreditation standards
- gst.gov.in — GST rates
- dgft.gov.in — DGFT import/export notifications
- meity.gov.in — IT/electronics policy
- cdsco.gov.in — medical device regulations
- cpcb.nic.in — pollution control (relevant for some disinfection claims)

Use for: industry news, deployment case studies (Indian context preferred), commentary.

- YourStory (yourstory.com)

- Economic Times Hospitality (hospitality.economictimes.indiatimes.com)
- Economic Times HealthWorld (health.economictimes.indiatimes.com)
- Economic Times Retail (retail.economictimes.indiatimes.com)
- Livemint (livemint.com)
- Business Standard (business-standard.com)
- The Hindu BusinessLine (thehindubusinessline.com)
- Moneycontrol (moneycontrol.com)
- HospitalityNet (hospitalitynet.org)
- HealthcareITNews India
- Robotics & Automation News (roboticsandautomationnews.com)
- The Robot Report (therobotreport.com)

## Tier 5 — academic / research

Use for: technical claims, efficiency studies, research-backed statements.

- Papers indexed on Google Scholar with a verifiable DOI
- IEEE Xplore
- ACM Digital Library
- arXiv (cs.RO category) — mark as preprint when cited

## Tier 0 — off-limits (never cite)

- Wikipedia — use as a starting point for research, never as a citation
- AI-generated summaries (ChatGPT, Perplexity output) — never cite
- Competitor blogs — never cite a competitor as a source of a fact
- Any site without a clearly dated article
- Press releases more than 3 years old (unless citing the date of a historical event)
- Quora, Reddit, Medium personal posts (unless the author has a verifiable industry credential and the post is clearly opinion, not fact)

---

## Citation format in posts

Every citation in a post follows this pattern:

> According to the [Federation of Hotel & Restaurant Associations of India](https://fhrai.com/), Indian hotel occupancy reached 68% in FY 2024-25.

- Link text: the source's name.
- URL: the exact article or page supporting the claim.
- Rewrite the fact in your own words; never copy-paste.
- Never chain two quotes from the same source in one post.

## Updating this list

Adding a new source:

1. Verify the domain publishes original, dated content.
2. Verify it's not a content mill or SEO farm.
3. Open a PR that adds it to the appropriate tier with a one-line note on why.
4. Link to two example articles showing the quality level expected.

Removing a source:

1. If a source publishes inaccurate or stale content, move it to Tier 0 with a note.
2. Keep it in Tier 0 (don't delete) so past posts' citations remain traceable.
