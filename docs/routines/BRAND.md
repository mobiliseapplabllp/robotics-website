## 2. Voice & tone

- **Voice**: business-direct, confidence without hype, numbers over adjectives.
- **Audience**: Indian enterprise buyers — CFOs, COOs, facility heads, hotel GMs, hospital administrators.
- **First-person plural** ("we at Mobilise") is fine but use sparingly; third-person about MALL reads better in most contexts.
- **Humour**: rare, dry, never at a customer's expense.

## 4. Claims that are OFF-LIMITS

- Never use: "world-first", "India's first", "only", "best", "leading" — unless there is a specific, citable source supporting the exact phrasing.
- Never claim a customer by name without their written permission on file.
- Never quote a specific ROI number without citing the pilot, study, or source.
- Never claim a regulatory certification the specific product variant sold in India does not carry.
- Never reference Keenon's global customer logos as if they were MALL's customers.

## 6. Do's and don'ts in prose

**Do**:
- Name specific Keenon models (DINERBOT T10, KLEENBOT C40).
- State INR pricing bands only when they come from an approved price card (never exact quotes on the public site).
- Reference Indian cities, states, regulations by name.
- Use lakhs and crores for amounts above a lakh.
- Lead sections with the direct answer before the reasoning.

**Don't**:
- Use banned phrases listed in `BLOG-STYLE.md`.
- Describe MALL as Keenon's exclusive India partner unless the agreement explicitly says "exclusive".
- Translate Keenon marketing copy verbatim — always rewrite in India-addressed voice.
- Invent testimonials, quotes, or stats.
