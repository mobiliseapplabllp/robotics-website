## LinkedIn (1,200–1,800 chars; first-person MALL voice; 3 hashtags)

Hotel GMs in India keep asking us the same thing: "Can a robot actually do concierge work in my lobby — and what does it cost?"

The honest answer is yes, partially, and meaningfully cheaper than two extra shifts.

A KEENON G1 in the lobby plus a W3 on the room floors is the configuration we deploy most for 120–180 key business and upper-mid-market properties in India. The G1 greets, recognises returning VIPs, escorts in 40+ languages, and integrates into your PMS. The W3 picks up amenity drops and mini-bar restocks the runner used to do at 11pm.

CapEx band before GST: ₹18–28 lakh for the two-robot stack. AMC year-two onward: ₹1.5–2.2 lakh. Operating cost: about ₹50,000 a year. Payback for a property running a two-shift concierge desk and a late-night runner: 18–30 months on conservative assumptions.

What this post walks through, with numbers stated in plain INR and not buried under marketing:

— what the G1, W3, and S100 actually do in a lobby
— what could go wrong (lift integration, Wi-Fi blackspots, floor finish)
— how to handle GST, HSN, BIS, and AMC scope before signing
— the 60-day pilot pattern that has worked for every property we've shipped to

**3/** ROI math for a 120–180 key property:

CapEx (pre-GST): ₹18–28 lakh
AMC y2+: ₹1.5–2.2 lakh
Op cost: ~₹50k/year
Labour displaced: ~₹6.96 lakh/year (1 concierge shift + 1 runner shift)

Simple cash payback: 18–30 months.

**4/** What goes wrong in real Indian deployments (in order):

1. Lift OEM integration drag
2. Lobby Wi-Fi blackspots
3. Floor finish — polished travertine and deep-pile rugs both fail SLAM
4. Activating 40 languages when you actually need 4

Solve at the building, not the robot.

**5/** Procurement notes Indian buyers actually need:

— GST + HSN line documented on quote, not after
— AMC scope in writing from year two
— Pilot 60 days before fleet commit
— Two reference customers + one reference deployment that didn't work
